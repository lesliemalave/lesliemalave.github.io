Assignment 1

Generative Art is a process of algorithmically generating new ideas, forms, shapes, colors or patterns. 

  • You must first create rules that provide boundaries for the creation process. 

  • Then a computer follows those rules to produce new works on your behalf.  

  • Computers can generate thousands of ideas in milliseconds

Generative Art Example:

Georg Nees - Schotter (Gravel), 1968

A row of 12 squared gradually increases magnitude of randomness in location and rotation.

Chart Critique

  • Contrasting information in the title vs the legend. Is this info from 2017 & 2018? Or 2016 & 2017?

  • The data labels on the bars are horizontal - must rotate head to be able to read

  • Data labels are redundant if the Y axis has the dollar cut off information

  • Lastly, more contrasting colors could have been used (e.g a darker blue).
